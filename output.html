OVERVIEW

The number of information technology projects continues to grow while their complexity increases. Project management is an important skill to master not only for project managers but also for project team members. This class will cover theoretical and practical aspects of project management. Topics include project integration management, organizational theory, group behavior, project management skills such as scope, time, and cost management, criteria for project success and reasons for failure, human resource management of individuals and teams, and communication. The class will work in teams to accomplish a real project utilizing the skills of project management.

PROJECT TEAM MEETINGS

Doing a team project and learning about an effective process for how your team can work together is an important part of this class. Your team will not only learn the tools and techniques of project management (e.g. how to do a Gantt chart) but will also apply these learnings to your team process.  Your team should set up your own meetings including time and frequency.

The team meetings are important. Not only will you use these meetings to plan and work on your projects, and do your assignments, but you will also discuss the course lecture topics.

 

PROJECT TEAM PARTICIPATION

Your participation in the project is essential.  Team success depends on everyone in the team giving it their best. We will be checking your team participation through your work in Google docs, and other means.  Majority of your grade is based on group work. This means attending meetings, communicating between meetings, and working on and contributing to the project. If you do not participate and your team colleagues do the work for you, then this means you are what is called a free rider (benefiting from others' work). Therefore, if it turns out that you have not participated sufficiently in your team project, then I will lower your grade. Be a team player.

ATTENDANCE AND DISCUSSIONS

This is a remote class, but lectures will be synchronous over zoom. You will be expected to keep up with the readings and lectures if you are not able to attend the lectures in real-time.  You will be expected to contribute to the weekly class discussion assignments in the Canvas DISCUSSION FORUM (not Ed Discussion).  Note there will be individual as well as project team based discussions. You will be able to access the class Discussion forums on the left navigation bar. You will be graded on your participation in these discussion topics. If you participate in the discussion for each class topic, then you will receive full points for your participation grade. If you do not participate in each discussion session, then you will receive points based on how much you have contributed. Stating "I agree" or "This is good", or "Good idea" does not constitute good quality discussion. You need to provide a thoughtful response about the class material.
